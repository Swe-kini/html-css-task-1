Project

The Crop Protection System is an innovative project that utilizes computer vision and IoT technologies to detect and identify specific animals in real-time. The project is implemented on a Raspberry Pi, making it a cost-effective and accessible solution for animal detection and monitoring.
The system leverages a camera connected to the Raspberry Pi to capture live video footage. Through the integration of computer vision algorithms and a pre-trained deep learning model, the system can identify specific animals, such as elephants and cows in the captured video stream. To enhance the usability and effectiveness of the system, additional features have been incorporated. When an animal of interest is detected, the system plays a deterrent sound to discourage the animal's presence. Furthermore, an SMS notification is sent using the Twilio service, providing real-time alerts to the user about the detected animal. The project also integrates a PIR (Passive Infrared) sensor, which enables the system to operate selectively based on motion detection. By utilizing the PIR sensor, the camera is activated only when motion is detected, conserving power and reducing unnecessary video processing.
The Crop Protection System offers several advantages, including its versatility, low- cost implementation, and potential for various applications. It can be deployed in wildlife conservation, agricultural settings, or even for home security purposes to detect and monitor specific animal species.
